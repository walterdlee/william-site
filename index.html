Famous People with Disabilities

will.i.am

Musician · Producer · Tech Entrepreneur

Living with ADHD

by Walter Lee

Background

Photo: Charley Gallay / Getty Images Entertainment, via Biography.com (Biography.com Editors, 2020)

Born

William James Adams Jr., March 15, 1975, in Los Angeles — raised in the Estrada Courts housing projects, Boyle Heights (East LA). (National Today, n.d.)

Family

will.i.am was raised solely by his mother, Debra, and has never met his father. Growing up, his mother made him wear suits to school, saying “You ain’t going to school with no play clothes, you’re going to school to learn.” (The Diary of a CEO, 2023)

School

His mother enrolled him in Palisades Charter High School, where he met Allan Pineda (apl.de.ap) — steering him away from local gang activity.

Turning point

Was performing with a rap group by the eighth grade. That group evolved into the Black Eyed Peas, formed in 1995 with apl.de.ap and Taboo. (AllMusic, n.d.; Biography.com Editors, 2020)

Music

The Black Eyed Peas

will.i.am, apl.de.ap, and Taboo had formed a group called Atban Klann previously and were a part of LA’s hip-hop circuit. The Black Eyed Peas grew to global stardom after Fergie joined the group in 2002, as the group began releasing hits such as “Where Is The Love?” (playing above). The group continued to push out hits throughout the 2000s such as “Let’s Get It Started,” “Boom Boom Pow,” and “I Gotta Feeling.” The Peas have sold more than 76 million albums and won six Grammy Awards. (AllMusic, n.d.; Biography.com Editors, 2020; Scavieli, 2024)

Solo work and production

Throughout his music career as a member of The Black Eyed Peas, will.i.am also released music as a solo artist. He has released Lost Change (2001), Songs About Girls (2007), and #willpower (2013), the last built around collaborations with Justin Bieber, Miley Cyrus, and Britney Spears. will.i.am also boasts an extensive catalogue as a producer, with work done for names such as Michael Jackson, Britney Spears, and Usher. (AllMusic, n.d.; Biography.com Editors, 2020; Recording Academy, n.d.)

Television

He has served as a coach on The Voice UK and The Voice Australia, and has appeared on American Idol and The X Factor, making mentoring a public part of his career rather than a private one. (Biography.com Editors, 2020)

Mars music

In 2012 his song “Reach for the Stars” was broadcast from the surface of Mars by NASA’s Curiosity rover — the first song streamed from another planet, and a clear statement of where he wanted his music to sit relative to technology. (National Today, n.d.)

Business

i.am+

will.i.am is the founder of i.am+, a consumer electronics company aimed at wearables. Its releases included the Puls smartwatch and a line of iPhone accessories. (Biography.com Editors, 2020)

Business school

In October 2024, will.i.am graduated from Harvard Business School’s Owner/President Management program. Because he did not finish high school due to his emerging music career, this was his first time walking across a stage for graduation. (Dorisca, 2024)

i.am Angel Foundation

will.i.am launched the i.am Angel Foundation in 2009. This foundation funds STEAM education — science, technology, engineering, arts, and mathematics — along with college preparation and scholarships, particularly for the Boyle Heights neighborhood where he was raised. (National Today, n.d.)

ADHD

It’s hard to keep your attention, and you can’t sit still and you’re always moving and thinking about a whole bunch of things. But those traits work well for me in studios and in meetings about creative ideas. will.i.am, quoted in Understood.org

The early struggle. As a child he was known simply as “hyper.” Holding his attention on any one thing was difficult, and the restlessness that later became useful in a studio first showed up in a classroom, where it read as a problem to be corrected rather than a trait to be used. (The Understood Team, n.d.)

Turning it into an asset. The reframing came from noticing that the same traits behaved differently in different rooms. Difficulty sitting still and a mind that jumps between many things at once are obstacles in a classroom and advantages in a recording session or a creative meeting — and his parallel careers in music, television, and technology are, in effect, what a mind like that looks like when it is given enough to do. (The Understood Team, n.d.)

How he manages it. His answer is music itself. He has described it as a way to “keep my attention disorder in order,” saying it stops his mind from wandering because he can stay inside the music, and that it brings control to his thoughts. The tool and the career turn out to be the same thing. (The Understood Team, n.d.)

Talking about it openly. He has discussed his ADHD publicly and at length — including a 2023 long-form interview covering his creativity and mental health — which matters because a diagnosis that is usually described in terms of deficit is being described instead by someone who credits it for his work. His own summary of the approach: “For every obstacle, there’s some type of solution.” (The Diary of a CEO, 2023; The Understood Team, n.d.)
